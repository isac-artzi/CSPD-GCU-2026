Agent / AI Agent

Core Concept

A system that perceives its environment, reasons about it, and takes actions autonomously to achieve goals. Unlike a simple chatbot that only responds when asked, an agent can plan multi-step tasks and use tools.

In Your Classroom

An agent that reviews student lab reports — it reads the report, checks it against a rubric, provides feedback, and logs scores, all without step-by-step human guidance.

Math / Technical Detail

Agent behavior can be modeled as a policy π(a|s) — choosing action a given state s. The agent aims to maximize cumulative reward over time.

API (Application Programming Interface)

Core Concept Technical

A way for programs to talk to each other using structured requests and responses. APIs define what you can ask for and what format the answer comes in.

In Your Classroom

Your Streamlit app sends a student's question to Claude and gets a response back. The API handles all the communication behind the scenes.

Math / Technical Detail

Like a waiter taking your order (request) to the kitchen (server) and bringing back food (response). You don't need to know how to cook — just how to order.

Claude

Core Concept

Anthropic's AI assistant, built with a focus on safety and helpfulness. Claude is the large language model (LLM) we use throughout this workshop.

In Your Classroom

The "brain" behind the STEM tools we build today — it generates explanations, provides feedback, and answers student questions.

Math / Technical Detail

Think of Claude as a knowledgeable teaching assistant that never gets tired, can adapt to any subject, and is available 24/7.

Context Window

Technical

The maximum amount of text an LLM can process at once, including both the input (your prompt) and the output (the response). Everything the model "remembers" during a conversation must fit in this window.

In Your Classroom

Determines how much of a student's essay the AI can read at once. A larger context window means you can include more background material.

Math / Technical Detail

Measured in tokens; Claude's context window is ~200K tokens ≈ 150K words ≈ a 500-page book.

Embedding

Advanced

A numerical representation of text as a vector (list of numbers) in high-dimensional space. Similar meanings get mapped to nearby points.

In Your Classroom

Finding similar student questions to group them, or matching student work to relevant curriculum standards.

Math / Technical Detail

text → vector in ℝn where n ≈ 768–1536. Similar meanings → nearby vectors (measured by cosine similarity: cos θ = (A·B) / (|A||B|)).

Fine-tuning

Advanced

Additional training of a pre-trained model on a smaller, specific dataset to customize its behavior for a particular task or domain.

In Your Classroom

Customizing an AI to understand your school's specific rubric or grading standards (though for most classroom uses, prompt engineering is sufficient).

Math / Technical Detail

Adjusting model weights W using gradient descent on new training data: W_new = W_old - η∇L, where η is the learning rate and L is the loss function.

Hallucination

Core Concept

When an AI generates plausible-sounding but incorrect or fabricated information. The AI doesn't "know" it's wrong — it's producing statistically likely text.

In Your Classroom

Always verify AI-generated quiz answers and facts! Cross-reference important content before sharing with students.

Math / Technical Detail

The model predicts likely next tokens based on patterns, not truth. It's like a student who writes a confident-sounding essay based on vague memories — it might sound right but contain errors.

LLM (Large Language Model)

Core Concept

A neural network trained on massive text datasets to understand and generate human language. "Large" refers to billions of parameters (adjustable numbers) that encode patterns of language.

In Your Classroom

The core technology powering ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini — all the AI tools transforming education.

Math / Technical Detail

A function f(tokens) → probability distribution over the next token. Claude has billions of parameters (weights) learned during training.

Max Tokens

Technical

A parameter that sets the maximum number of tokens the AI will generate in its response. It acts as a ceiling — the response can be shorter but never longer.

In Your Classroom

Setting a word limit on AI responses (e.g., "Keep feedback under 200 words" combined with max_tokens=300).

Math / Technical Detail

The max_tokens parameter caps output length. In code: messages.create(max_tokens=1024).

Model

Core Concept

A specific version of an AI system with particular capabilities, speed, and cost characteristics (e.g., claude-sonnet-4-6, claude-haiku-4-5).

In Your Classroom

Choosing between faster/cheaper models for simple tasks (like formatting) or smarter models for complex tasks (like essay feedback).

Math / Technical Detail

Like choosing between a calculator and a computer — both compute, but with different capabilities and costs.

Prompt

Core Concept

The text input you give to an AI model. It can be a question, instruction, or any text you want the AI to respond to.

In Your Classroom

"Explain the water cycle for 7th graders" or "Generate 5 multiple-choice questions about the Civil War."

Math / Technical Detail

The more specific your prompt, the better the output — like giving clear instructions to a substitute teacher. "Teach science" vs. "Teach the parts of a cell to 9th graders using a city analogy."

Prompt Engineering

Core Concept

The skill of writing effective prompts to get desired AI behavior. This includes structuring instructions, providing examples, and specifying output format.

In Your Classroom

Crafting a system prompt that makes the AI grade essays the way you do — with your rubric, tone, and priorities.

Math / Technical Detail

Part art, part science — the practice of communicating effectively with AI to get the results you want.

Python

Technical

A beginner-friendly programming language widely used in data science, AI, and web development. Known for its readable, English-like syntax.

In Your Classroom

The language we use to build our Streamlit apps and call the Claude API. Also used in many STEM classrooms for data analysis.

Math / Technical Detail

Python reads almost like English: for student in classroom: print(student.name). That's why it's the most popular first programming language.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

Advanced

A technique that gives an AI access to external knowledge (documents, databases) before generating a response, reducing hallucinations and enabling domain-specific answers.

In Your Classroom

An agent that reads your course syllabus, textbook chapters, or past exams before generating a lesson plan — grounding its response in YOUR materials.

Math / Technical Detail

retrieve(query) → relevant_docs → LLM(query + docs) → response. The retrieval step uses embeddings and vector search to find relevant documents.

Streamlit

Technical

A Python framework that turns simple Python scripts into interactive web applications with minimal code. No HTML, CSS, or JavaScript knowledge required.

In Your Classroom

Creating a web interface for your AI teaching tools — a page where you or students can type questions and get AI-powered responses.

Math / Technical Detail

Streamlit is like a translator between Python and the web browser. Write st.button("Click me") and a real button appears on a webpage.

System Prompt

Core Concept

Special instructions given to the AI before the conversation begins that define its role, personality, constraints, and behavior throughout the entire interaction.

In Your Classroom

"You are a patient Socratic tutor for AP Chemistry students. Never give direct answers — instead, ask guiding questions." This shapes every response the AI gives.

Math / Technical Detail

Like a detailed job description for the AI — it sets expectations, defines the role, and establishes ground rules.

Temperature

Technical

A parameter controlling the randomness/creativity in AI responses. Ranges from 0 (deterministic, always picks the most likely word) to 1 (more creative and varied).

In Your Classroom

Low temperature (0.1–0.3) for quiz answers and factual content. Higher temperature (0.7–0.9) for creative writing prompts and brainstorming.

Math / Technical Detail

Applied to the softmax function: softmax(logits / T). Lower T → sharper probability distribution → more predictable output. At T→0, always picks the highest-probability token.

Token

Core Concept Technical

The smallest unit of text an LLM processes — roughly ¾ of a word in English. The AI reads and generates text as a sequence of tokens, not individual characters or whole words.

In Your Classroom

Understanding API costs (you pay per token) and context limits. A 1,000-word essay ≈ 1,333 tokens.

Math / Technical Detail

Text is split into tokens via BPE (Byte Pair Encoding). Example: "photosynthesis" → ["photo", "synthesis"]. Common words are single tokens; rare words are split into pieces.

Tool Use

Core Concept

An AI agent's ability to call external functions or services — like a calculator, web search, code interpreter, or database query — to accomplish tasks it can't do with language alone.

In Your Classroom

An AI that can actually solve math equations (using a calculator tool) rather than guessing, or one that can search your school's database for a student's previous grades.

Math / Technical Detail

Like giving a student a calculator during a math exam — the student still needs to know what to calculate, but the tool handles the computation.

Vector Database

Advanced

A specialized database designed to store and efficiently search through embeddings (vectors). Optimized for "find me things similar to X" queries.

In Your Classroom

Storing your entire curriculum — lesson plans, worksheets, past exams — so the AI can quickly find and reference relevant materials when answering questions.

Math / Technical Detail

Uses efficient nearest-neighbor search algorithms (like HNSW) to find similar vectors in high-dimensional space without comparing every single entry.
